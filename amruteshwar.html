Amruteshwar Temple
 Amruteshwar temple is a 1000 years old temple built in Hemadpanthi style of architecture with black stones. The temple is a beautiful getaway for pilgrims and historians who can explore the inside of the temple, which has intricate details to satisfy the history bugs in them. The sanctum in the temple is a labyrinthine structure with sculptures of Gods and Goddesses carved out of stone. There is a pond in the complex which adds to its overall beauty. It is one of the 12 temples built in 900 AD by King Jhang. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the temple has a lingam. The exquisite features of the temple, the hot spring that fills the sanctum with water during monsoons and the sculptures made of black stone makes the temple a must visit. One can also indulge in batting on the Arthur Lake on a visit to the temple.
Temple
Amruteshwar Temple is located at the start of the Ratanwadi village in the Igatpuri region. It is considered to be over 1,200 year old temple which features Lord Shiva as the main deity. This temple comes under Ahmednagar District and is surrounded by varied mountain ranges. Ratangad Fort is also located at a close distance from this old Lord Shiva Temple. The Amruteshwar Temple has been constructed in Hemadpanthi architectural style with beautiful rock carvings on the main shrine. Travelling by road to this temple is very difficult due to rough roads on the hills. Tourists can avail boats from Arthur Lake to visit this temple, which is situated approximately 8 km away from this lake.
